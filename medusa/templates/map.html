LAWS THAT PROTECTED WOMEN
Roe v. Wade (1973) — Overturned 2022
Guaranteed federal constitutional right to abortion for 49 years. Overturned by Dobbs v. Jackson in a 6-3 decision shaped by three Trump appointees. As of 2025, 21 states have banned or severely restricted abortion — including for rape and incest survivors.
Violence Against Women Act — VAWA (1994)
Funds DV shelters, rape crisis centers, and legal aid. Reauthorized through 2027. 2025 Trump budget proposed gutting its grant programs.
Title IX (1972) — Weakened 2025
Prohibits sex discrimination in federally funded education. Schools must respond to sexual assault. Trump administration rolled back protections in 2025.
Title VII — Civil Rights Act (1964)
Prohibits workplace sexual harassment and sex discrimination. The word "sex" was added by a conservative opponent who thought it would kill the bill. It passed anyway.
18 U.S.C. § 2261 — Interstate DV
Federal crime to cross state lines to commit domestic violence or violate a protective order.
LAWS THAT FAILED OR HARMED WOMEN
Equal Rights Amendment — Still Not Ratified
First introduced in 1923. Passed Congress in 1972. Needed 38 states to ratify — reached that number in 2020, but the deadline had passed. Opposition led by Phyllis Schlafly killed momentum in the 1970s-80s. As of 2025, women have no explicit equal rights guarantee in the US Constitution.
Child Marriage — Legal in Most States
As of 2024, only 8 states have banned child marriage with no exceptions. In remaining states, girls as young as 10 have been legally married to adult men with parental or judicial consent. Between 2000–2018, nearly 300,000 children were married in the US — the majority girls married to adult men.
Marital Rape — Legal Until 1993
Marital rape was not a crime in all 50 states until 1993. Many states still treat it as a lesser offense. Some retain spousal exemptions.
Comstock Act (1873) — Revived 2024
An 1873 obscenity law banning mailing contraception and abortion materials. Largely dormant for decades until Dobbs opened the door to revival. Trump DOJ signaled potential enforcement against mailed abortion medication.
Women's Bureau — Proposed Elimination 2025
Trump budget proposed eliminating the 105-year-old Women's Bureau, which tracked wage gaps, workplace violence, and administered VAWA grants covering 50M+ workers.
PROFESSIONS THAT EXCLUDED WOMEN
Law — Bradwell v. Illinois (1873)
The Supreme Court ruled states could bar women from practicing law. Myra Bradwell was denied a license in Illinois for being a woman. Women were not admitted to all US law schools until the 1970s.
Medicine — AMA Quotas Until 1970s
The AMA enforced informal quotas limiting women to ~5% of medical school enrollment through much of the 20th century. Women were routinely told medicine was unsuitable for them.
Military Combat — Banned Until 2016
Women were formally banned from direct ground combat until 2015–2016. Despite serving in war zones for decades, they were denied combat pay and promotions tied to combat experience.
Credit & Banking — Required Husband Until 1974
Before the Equal Credit Opportunity Act (1974), women could be refused a credit card, mortgage, or bank account without a male co-signer — regardless of their income.
Bartending — Goesaert v. Cleary (1948)
The Supreme Court upheld Michigan's law banning women from bartending unless their husband or father owned the bar. Not overturned until 1976.
Boston Marathon — Women Banned Until 1972
In 1967, Kathrine Switzer became the first woman to run with a race number. A race official physically attacked her mid-race trying to drag her off the course.
Computer Science — Deliberate Push-Out Since 1984
Women earned 37% of CS degrees in 1984. By 2023 that fell to ~18% — tied to the marketing of personal computers as boys' toys and systematic cultural exclusion from the field.
MEDICAL NEGLECT & HARM
Black Maternal Mortality
Black women die in childbirth at 2.6× the rate of white women — 69.9 per 100,000 live births vs 26.6. The majority of these deaths are preventable. Black women's pain is systematically undertreated and their symptoms dismissed.
Endometriosis — 7–10 Year Diagnosis Delay
Affects ~10% of women and girls. Average time to diagnosis: 7–10 years. Women are routinely told their pain is normal or psychosomatic. Causes infertility, organ damage, and debilitating chronic pain.
ER Wait Times — Gender Gap
Women wait an average of 65 minutes for pain medication in ERs vs 49 minutes for men with identical symptoms. Women are more likely to receive sedatives; men receive painkillers.
Forced Sterilization — Indian Health Service
Between 1970–1976, the IHS sterilized an estimated 25–50% of Native American women — often without informed consent, sometimes while under anesthesia for other procedures. A 1976 GAO investigation confirmed thousands of cases.
The Husband Stitch
Doctors adding extra sutures after childbirth to "tighten" the vagina for the husband's pleasure — performed without consent. Documented as recently as 2020 in US hospitals.
Non-Consensual Pelvic Exams in Medical Training
Standard practice at many US teaching hospitals: performing pelvic exams on anesthetized women without specific consent, as training exercises for medical students. Several states have now banned it.
Abortion Denial Post-Dobbs
Since 2022, documented cases of women denied emergency abortion care during miscarriage, ectopic pregnancy, and life-threatening complications. Women sent home to wait until "sick enough." Multiple deaths have been linked to care denial.
